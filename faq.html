Frequently Asked Questions

Join us at the Taninah Resort for the Tulum retreat - January 12-17, 2017

Is this retreat right for me?
  • Why should I go on this retreat?
    • If your interested in travel, adventure and self-reflection, this retreat is designed especially you. Nestled on a private, beautiful resort in the Caribbean, Taninah is an ideal setting for relaxation and rejuvenation.
  • Can I come alone?
    • Yes! Come alone or bring a friend. We are building a sisterhood that is fun, inclusive and supportive. You will be in great company and we will all have many opportunities to connect.
  • What can I expect to gain from this retreat?
    • We expect you to be open to a unique adventure. You can expect to reflect and gain clarity into your motivations and goals. Prepare to be empowered to challenge yourself in 2017.
  • How many people will be there?
    • We expect a sold out group of 10-12 women who are ready to step up to the challenge of trying something new.
  • What are the retreat leaders like?
    • We are fun and adventurous foodies, who have been traveling, planning, and visioning together for several years. We are inspired to explore the connections of sisterhood, health, culture and travel. We invite you to become our traveling companions!
    • Amy Lee is a creative free spirit. She is a graphic designer with a passion for technology, maps, yoga and design. She has a background in international business, fine arts and programming.
    • Nikki Jay loves all things fashion, is a Certified Health Education Specialist, jewelry designer and Latin dance instructor. She has a background in women’s studies, psychology, biology and health education.
  • What makes this retreat unique?
    • We have developed a exclusive collection of workshops, activities and experiences. Watch the sunrise while doing yoga on the beach, visit historic Mayan ruins, float in a crystal clear cenote and participate in team-building adventures.

      • Yes. Because we will travel outside the U.S., each guest will need to have a valid passport to come on this trip. We recommend reviewing the United States Postal Services guidelines and timelines for obtaining your passport.. If you need a new or renewed passport, please do not delay applying for this. Note: we encourage all guests who will come on this trip to have their passports in hand by September 2016.

      • We've booked two shuttles for our groups arrivals. One at noon and another at 6:pm EST. Please plan to book your arrival flight to Cancun International Airport (CUN) on Thursday, January 11, 2017 with enough time to secure your luggage and make the shuttle.

      What type of activities are planned?
      • What if I’ve never done yoga?
        • If you have never done yoga, that is okay! The yoga classes provided will be suitable for all levels, beginner to advanced. Yoga classes provided are Vinyasa, which focus on meditative breathing and repetitive movements to relax the body and quiet the mind.
        Will we do any strenuous physical activities?
        • Yes, a few activities will be strenuous in nature, but guests are able to choose their level of involvement. For instance, climbing the ruins, swimming at the beach and in the cenote, and yoga on the beach may require endurance. We are asking that you are open to participating in addition to knowing and responding to your own limits.

            What type of currancy is needed and are ATM's available?
            • What is the local currency and exchange rate?
              • Please see the “Universal Currency Calculator” from the Mexico Tourism Board to calculate current exchange and currency rates: http://www.visitmexico.com/en/mexico-currency-and-exchange.
            • Should I bring cash and/or traveler’s checks?
              • We recommend you bringing a combination of U.S. dollars, Mexican Peso's and a credit/debit card. Pesos and U.S. dollars may be the preferred currency depending on the restaurant/vendor
            • Are ATMs readily available?
              • There are no ATMs located at Taninah, but there are ATMs readily available in all of the surrounding areas (for a fee per use). We advise you to avoid carrying an abundance of money with you and, as always, please use reasonable safety precautions when using ATMs and handling money in public.

                  • What type of weather can I expect?

Dress for comfort.
