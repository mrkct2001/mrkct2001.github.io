Frequently Asked Questions


Join us at the Taninah Resort for the Tulum retreat - January 12-17, 2017

Is this retreat right for me?
  • Why should I go on this retreat?
    • If you're interested in travel, adventure and self-reflection, this retreat is designed especially for you. Nestled on a private, beautiful resort in the Caribbean, Taninah is an ideal setting for relaxation and rejuvenation.
    • Can I come alone?
      • Yes! Come alone or bring a friend. We are building a sisterhood that is fun, inclusive and supportive. You will be in great company and we will all have many opportunities to connect.
      • What can I expect to gain from this retreat?
        • We expect you to be open to a unique adventure. You can expect to reflect and gain clarity into your motivations and goals. Prepare to be empowered to challenge yourself in 2017.
        • How many people will be there?
          • We expect a sold out group of 10-12 women who are ready to step up to the challenge of trying something new.
          • What are the retreat leaders like?
            • We are fun and adventurous foodies, who have been traveling, planning, and visioning together for several years. We are inspired to explore the connections of sisterhood, health, culture and travel. We invite you to become our traveling companions!
              • Amy Lee is a creative free spirit. She is a graphic designer with a passion for technology, maps, yoga and design. She has a background in international business, fine arts and programming.
                • Nikki Jay loves all things fashion, is a Certified Health Education Specialist, jewelry designer and Latin dance instructor. She has a background in women’s studies, psychology, biology and health education.
                • What makes this retreat unique?
                  • We have developed a exclusive collection of workshops, activities and experiences. Watch the sunrise while doing yoga on the beach, visit historic Mayan ruins, float in a crystal clear cenote and participate in team-building adventures.

                  •  

                  What about Registration?
                  • How long is the retreat?
                    • This retreat is six days and five nights.
                    • When should I book this trip?
                      • We encourage you to reserve your spot as soon as possible. We'd love everyone to come but we are expecting a sold out retreat and it's first come first served. To secure your spot, you can either pay a deposit or pay in full. Deposits for early bird packages are due by July 15, 2016. For the regular payment option, or the pay in full option, your deposit or full payment will be due on August 1, 2016.
                        • Please see the Register page for payment schedules and costs. Don’t delay!
                        • How much does the retreat cost and when will I need to submit payment?
                          • The price of the retreat is $1,600. However, we offer you a substantial savings (10%). You are able to choose the best option to suit your needs. See the Register page for a breakdown of pricing and payment deadlines for each option.
                          • What is included in the retreat fee?
                            • We are staying at an all-inclusive resort. Your fees cover:

                              Gratuity for on-site staff will be present 24 / 7. Exclusive use of the 10-acre, private property (see map). Chef-prepared meals - daily breakfast, lunch, and dinner. Sisters with Passports workshops and materials. Round-trip airport transfer to Taninah. Round trip transportation and admission to Xplor Fuego, Coba, Xph Ha' Beach and the Cenote. Sunrise Yoga

                              Please refer to the "How will I get from the airport and from the retreat space?" question for additional information about ground transportation parameters.
                          • What is not included in retreat fee?
                            • Your flight is not included in the retreat fee. Although meals will be provided daily while you are at Taninah; however, on Day 3 you will have the opportunity to explore off-site. If you choose to venture out of the resort area to explore, you will need to bring additional money to purchase off-site meals, souvenirs, and other items and services (such as massage).

                          What about Lodging, Travel and Transportation?
                        • Will I need to secure my own flight?
                          • Yes. Travel to Mexico (flight) is not included in the cost of your retreat, so you must reserve and purchase your own round-trip flight. We encourage you to do this as soon as possible after booking your spot on the retreat.
                          • What amenities are available at the resort?
                            • Please see this link from Taninah for a detailed list of amenities.
                            • Can I have my own room?
                              • Due to the unique features of this retreat, most guest bungalows have three to four beds. If you are interested in booking a single or double room, please book this option soon as accommodations are first come, first served. Please visit the Register page to review and book accommodations.
                                • Why Taninah?
                                  • This unique vacation property, Taninah Resort, has been featured as a must-visit travel destination in HGTV’s Extreme Homes and The Travel Channel’s Amazing Vacation Homes. It's also been featured in the magazines Caribbean World and Travel + Leisure. For additional information about Taninah, visit http://www.taninah.com/home.php.
                                  • Where is Taninah?
                                    • Taninah Resort is located in the municipality of Tulum, Quintana Roo, Mexico in Mexico’s Riviera Maya in the Yucatan Peninsula. Tulum is on the Caribbean coast, south of Florida on the south side of the Gulf of Mexico. Tulum features sandy sprawling beaches and neighbors the Bahamas, Jamaica, and Cuba. Taninah is just one hour south of Cancun, three hours north Belize and a 45 minute ferry ride to Cozumel.
                                    • Will I need my passport?
                                      • Of course! We are Sisters with Passports and we passionately encourage globe-trotting. As we will travel outside of U.S. shores, each guest will need to have a valid passport to come on this trip. Review the United States Postal Service guidelines and timelines for obtaining your passport. We recommend that all guests have their passports in hand by September 2016.
                                      • When should my flights arrive to and depart from the Cancun International Airport for this retreat?
                                        • We've booked two shuttles for our groups arrivals. One at 2pm EST and another at 6pm EST. Please plan to book your arrival flight to Cancun International Airport (CUN) on Thursday, January 12, 2017 with enough time to secure your luggage and make the shuttle.

                                        • We've also booked two shuttles for our group's departures. One at 7am EST and another at 12pm EST. Please plan to book your departure flight from Cancun International Airport (CUN) on Tuesday, January 17, 2017 accordingly. Note: Taninah is approximately 1 - 1.5 hours from CUN.
                                        • How will I get to and from the airport and Taninah Retreat?
                                          • Taninah staff will be arranging ground transportation via STP Caribe. This is included in your resort fee, however, if you are unable to make these times, individual transportation can be arranged for approximately $100 each way, at your expense.
                                          • How will we get around once we’re in Mexico?
                                            • Transportation to retreat activity locations (Xpu Ha' Beach, Coba and Xplor Fuego) is included in the retreat fee. If other transportation needs arise, please let us know as we can work with the retreat space to help coordinate the travel. These transportation costs will be additional to you and will not be covered in the retreat fee.

                                            •  

                                            What type of activities are planned?
                                          • What if I’ve never done yoga?
                                            • Never done yoga? That's totally okay! The yoga sessions provided will be suitable for all levels, beginner to advanced. The yoga sessions will be Vinyasa, which focus on meditative breathing and repetitive movements to relax the body and quiet the mind.
                                              Will we do any strenuous physical activities?
                                              • We do encourage you to challenge yourself and we will challenge you. However, you will be able to choose your own level of involvement. For instance, climbing the Mayan ruins at Coba, riding a zipline at Xplor Fuego, and sunrise yoga on the beach will require stamina. We ask that you stay open to participating while knowing and responding to your own limits.

                                              Dining options
                                              • What foods will be provided?
                                                • Authentic, local Mayan meals will be prepared daily, by hand, by our on-site Mayan chef. There will be vegetarian and non-vegetarian options available. Taninah's creative menus are all made with love and local chemical-free produce. You will enjoy fresh-caught fish, organic chicken and seasonal fruits like mango, papaya, and pineapple. Flavorful meals will include a variety of delicious signature salads that incorporate jicama, pumpkin seeds, and chia seeds will leave you satisfied, yet feeling lighter and cleaner. For more information about the menu and beverage options, please visit: http://www.taninah.com/meals.php.

                                                •  

                                                What about internet connectivity, mobile phone service and electricity?
                                                • Will I have internet or phone service at the retreat space?
                                                  • Unfortunately, there is not resort-wide wifi access on-site. If you must access the internet, please note that the closest internet cafe is at Puerto Aventuras, which is about 15 minutes north of Taninah. Please be open to being “unplugged” and fully engaging in the retreat while enjoying the beautiful surroundings. According to Taninah, most guests find that they can use their mobile phones with little to no problem. If you wish to have mobile phone service, please check with your carrier prior to the trip to make sure that you have coverage in Mexico.

                                                  •  

                                                  What type of currency is needed and are ATM's available?
                                                  • What is the local currency and exchange rate?
                                                    • Please see the “Universal Currency Calculator” from the Mexico Tourism Board to calculate current exchange and currency rates: http://www.visitmexico.com/en/mexico-currency-and-exchange.
                                                    • Should I bring cash and/or traveler’s checks?
                                                      • We recommend you bringing a combination of U.S. dollars, Mexican peso's and a credit / debit card. Pesos and U.S. dollars may be the preferred currency depending on the restaurant / vendor.
                                                      • Are ATMs readily available?
                                                        • There are no ATMs located at Taninah, but there are ATMs readily available in the surrounding areas. Please use reasonable safety precautions when using ATMs and handling money in public.

                                                        •  

                                                        Insurance?
                                                        • Is travel insurance recommended?
                                                          • Yes, travel insurance is highly recommended and is not covered in the cost of your retreat. We encourage every guest to purchase travel insurance to provide protection for protect travel arrangements / flight, medical, baggage protection and worldwide emergency assistance. Sisters with Passports will not be held responsible for servicing and / or refunding reservations affected by acts of God, wildfires, accidental fire, lightning strike, or any other major cause outside of Sisters with Passports' immediate control.

                                                          • Are there any health or safety concerns I should know about?
                                                          • What immunizations or vaccinations are recommended for traveling to Mexico?
                                                            • Please visit the CDC’s Health Information for Travelers to Mexico for up-to-date information.
                                                            • What if I’m pregnant, can I come on this retreat?
                                                              • Due to the amount of physical activity during this retreat, if you are expecting we recommend you join us on our next trip.
                                                              • How safe is the area we’re staying?
                                                                • The state of Quinatana Roo has no travel warnings or safety advisories in effect. For additional information, visit the U.S. Embassy and Consulates in Mexico website.
                                                                • Is the water safe to drink?
                                                                  • We recommend the purified drinking water available at the resort, which is included in the cost of the retreat. In addition, a beverage package will be provided which includes soda, national beer, white wine, red wine, purified ice, daily fresh-squeezed orange juice (when available), morning and evening coffee and Mexican hot chocolate. If guests wish to have additional options, beverage options are available for purchase.

                                                                  •  

                                                                  What should I pack?
                                                                  • What should I bring?
                                                                    • Check out the recommendations in "The Ultimate Packing List" from SmarterTravel. You must bring your valid passport! See the "Will I Need My Passport?" section for additional information. Some other things we encourage you to bring with you: an attitude of adventure, insect repellent, sunglasses, a hat or two, a variety of clothing; including long-sleeved shirts, long pants, swimwear, closed-toed comfortable walking shoes or sneakers, and water shoes.
                                                                  • What type of weather can I expect?
                                                                    • In January, the weather is typically in the low to mid-80s (degrees Fahrenheit) during the day, but evenings will be cooler. We suggest you pack some long-sleeved shirts and long pants. Dress for comfort.

                                                                    •  

                                                                    Cancellations?
                                                                    • How can I cancel my reservation?
                                                                      • If for any reason you will need to cancel your reservation, you will be able to do so prior to September 1, 2016. However, due to expenses to secure the accommodations and activity spaces, we will not be able to return your deposit. Canceling after September 1, 2016 may not allow enough time to secure another guest to fill your spot. If you have booked your spot and will no longer be able to attend, please contact us as soon as possible so that we are able to make other arrangements.
